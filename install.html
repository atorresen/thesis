These wires were folded by makers of math and art.

In "make math with art" workshops, participants folded wires according to the Kolakoski sequence.

The Kolakoski sequence (1, 2, 2, 1, 1, 2, 1, 2, 2, 1, 2, 2, 1, 1, ...) was proposed by the artist William Kolakoski, who was a trained painter and a recreational mathematician.

The Kolakoski sequence alternates between blocks of 1s and blocks of 2s defined by the 1s and 2s within the sequence. The sequence is self-referential and self-generating; it defines itself by describing itself. The sequence is constructed entirely with the number 1, the number 2, and a simple rule that induces a back-and-forth movement between numbers and the quantities they represent.

The rhythms that propel the Kolakoski sequence forward are best felt by building them with your own hands. The wires assembled here are artifacts of tactile experiences of learning/doing/feeling math.
