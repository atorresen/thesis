This is a project that builds art with math. This is a project that builds math with art.
This is a project that wonders if art and math are made of the same stuff, and what that stuff is, and how we might invite others to grow to love it.

1, 2, 2, 1, 1, 2, 1, 2, 2, 1, 2, 2, 1, 1, …

The wires in this installation are artifacts of a series of math/art workshops where people were invited to fold wire according to a mathematical pattern: the Kolakoski sequence.

Each piece of wire holds a rhythm. Each piece of wire holds a process.
Together, the wires form a tangle, a cloud, a cage, an entryway.
Each piece of wire holds some art. Each piece of wire holds some math.

math, art, art, math, math, art, math, art, art, math, art, art, math, math, …

Bouncing between art and math, and art with math, and math with art, we arrive not in the middle, but in a new place. We belong here, and our confusion and our feelings about being here belong here too. We build a room where everyone is invited.
